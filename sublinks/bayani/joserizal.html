Dr. Jose Rizal

Jose Rizal was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines, which was then a colony of Spain. His parents were Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso, both of whom were educated and belonged to prominent families. Rizal was the seventh of eleven children. In 1872, Rizal witnessed the execution of three Filipino priests who were accused of sedition. This event had a profound impact on him and made him aware of the injustices and oppression that the Filipinos experienced under the Spanish colonial government. He pursued his studies and eventually went to study medicine in Spain, where he also became involved in the Propaganda Movement, a group of Filipino expatriates who advocated for reforms in the Philippines. In 1887, Rizal published his first novel, "Noli Me Tangere," which exposed the abuses and corruption of the Spanish colonial government and clergy in the Philippines.

The book was banned in the Philippines but gained widespread popularity and inspired many Filipinos to join the movement for independence. In 1891, Rizal published his second novel, "El Filibusterismo," which continued to expose the injustices of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic Church. In 1892, Rizal returned to the Philippines and established the La Liga Filipina, a civic organization that aimed to unite Filipinos and promote reforms peacefully. However, the organization was disbanded after Rizal's arrest and exile to Dapitan, where he continued to practice medicine and engage in agricultural activities. In 1896, the Philippine Revolution broke out, and Rizal was implicated in the rebellion. He was arrested, tried, and convicted of sedition, rebellion, and conspiracy. On December 30, 1896, Rizal was executed by firing squad in Manila. His death sparked widespread outrage and led to the intensification of the revolution. Rizal's life and writings continue to inspire Filipinos and people around the world to fight for justice, freedom, and national sovereignty. He is now regarded as a national hero in the Philippines, and his life and works are celebrated every year on Rizal Day, which falls on December 30.
