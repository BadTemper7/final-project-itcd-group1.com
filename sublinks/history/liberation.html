THE LIBERATION OF THE PHILIPPINES FROM JAPANESE

Gen. MacArthur, who had promised to return, landed in Leyte in 1944, thus commencing the military campaign to liberate the Philippines from the Japanese. In the ensuing struggle, Manila and most of the major Philippine cities suffered grievous damage. MacArthur declared the military campaign on Luzon closed on July 4, 1945, but the bulk of the Japanese ground forces were still intact in the mountains. Fighting continued in Mindanao.
