Yamang Tubig

Any major collection of water, typically covering the surface of a planet like Earth, is referred to as a body of water. A body of water need not be enclosed or immobile. A case in point is The largest and deepest body of water is the ocean. Another example is a river, which is a long, narrow body of water that runs towards the sea and has salty water. Wherever water travels from one location to another, such as up a mountain or hill, they are also regarded as bodies of water.
