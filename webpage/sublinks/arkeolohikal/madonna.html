Madonna

Vicente Manansala is a Filipino painter who was born in Macabebe, Pampanga, on January 22, 1920. He was in the vanguard of the modernist movement in the country as a member of the Thirteen Moderns and the Neo-Realists. With the exception of a few abstract paintings, Manansala's art was always figurative. Manansala's paintings have been recognized as masterpieces that fused the barrio andcity cultures. "Delicate tones, contours, and patterns of person and environment are expertly juxtaposed," Manansala invented transparent cubism. The "Madonna of the Slums" is one of his most famous artwork masterpieces.The Madonna of the Slums were created by Manansala in the 1950 using a transparent cubism style. The scene in the Madonna of the Slums, like most of Manansala's other masterpieces, was inspiredby the streets of Manila after the war. The painting shows a woman and child from the rural who moved to the city and experienced poverty in postwar Manila. The artwork depicts the difficulties faced by many Filipinos while living in the slums. It conveys the sense that the lives of persons who reside in this place are unhappy, poor, and unhealthy. Manansala emphasized the mother's and child's melancholy facial expressions, which are mirrored by the typical slum scene of extreme poverty. The background depicts the typical day in the lives of a slum dweller.
