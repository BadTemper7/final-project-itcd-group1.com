In earth science and geology, a landform or physical feature is made up of a geomorphological unit, and it is frequently identified by its surface characteristics and geographic location as a topographical element. A province and domain of geology, landforms also include seascapes and features of ocean water bodies such as bays, estuaries, seas, etc., as well as land in water such as submerged mountains and volcanoes and enormous ocean basins that lay beneath of thin water..
